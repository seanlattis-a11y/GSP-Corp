How Enhanced Geothermal Systems work

EGS engineers permeability in hot rock, then circulates fluid to harvest heat for power or process energy.

Talk to GPS Our Services

The process

  1. Access the resource: Drill into hot crystalline basement with well designs for high temperature and integrity.
  2. Create & characterize permeability: Stimulation to generate/connect fractures; quantify with pressure response, flow allocation, tracers, microseismic and fiber diagnostics.
  3. Circulate & harvest heat: Inject, heat‑exchange, produce at adjacent well; surface conversion (often binary/ORC), then reinject.

What has to go right

  • Drilling & well integrity — manage hard‑rock ROP, losses, tool survivability; maintain isolation under thermal cycling.
  • Zonal control & stimulation effectiveness — place treatment where intended; achieve transmissivity without exceeding seismicity constraints.
  • Connectivity & sweep — distributed flow paths and meaningful heat‑exchange volume.
  • Sustained circulation & mass balance — stable operation with acceptable recovery/losses.
  • Induced seismicity management — monitoring arrays, thresholds and response protocols.
